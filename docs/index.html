The Supreme Court of Israel

Functions of the Court

The court has original and final judicial review of the governmentâ€™s actions. It also acts as court of appeals for criminal, civil and administrative cases. The Israeli Supreme Court hears thousands of petitions and appeals yearly and issues 1,500 reasoned decisions. Most of the cases are adjudicated by random panels of three justices.

Justices

The 15 justices are appointed by a committee composed of members of the parliament (two members), government (two members), Supreme Court (three justices) and the Israeli bar (two members). To be appointed to the Supreme Court a candidate must receive 7 out 9 votes. This condition leads to inside "deals" within the committee that are supposed to staisfy different fractions within it. The Chief Justice is appointed through a seniority meachnism, to avoid rivary and politicization of the court. There are serious doubts if this system produces said result.

The Current Chief Justice - Justice Esther Hayut

The Chief Justice has an important role in navigating the court and the entire judicial system. Justice Esther Hayut started her term as Chief Justice recently, on Octobe 26, 2017, on the day of the retiremnt of the former Chief Justice, Miryam Naor. Since the Chief Justice is appointed by seniority and not merit, we have no idea whether she will be a good leader and manager. She is considered to be a tough justice but a prinicipled one. Her best known fields are commercial and private law. She is the third women to be appointed Chief Justice.
